Introduction

We developed a bilingual website (Hindi and English versions) named “Aanchal” and deployed our Risk Prediction Model as a Web App on the website. “Aanchal” which signifies ‘A Safe Shelter’ was chosen as the brand name to highlight the importance of early diagnosis for better prognosis.⁠

The website has a brief introduction to Breast Cancer and highlights the importance of early screening. A click button takes the subject to the risk prediction page where the subject can feed in their details and acquire their results instantly in a bilingual mode.

The AANCHAL App

For audio-to-risk prediction, we developed an Android app which takes in the audio either as live audio or as recorded audio and converts that into Hindi text, followed by English text followed by feature extraction to perform the risk prediction which shows up as the output as similar to the web app.
‍
⁠All the intermediate files generated alongside the final results (Hindi text and English text) can be read and saved by the subject on the phone. The Android app is built on similar lines to the website and has been named as “Aanchal”.

Machine Learning (ML) Model for risk prediction

Two complete datasets (with control and cases) were received for training and testing the ML model. The larger dataset which dated back to last year’s data collection with 628 cases and 1622 controls was used for training and validation purposes; while ML model prediction results were confirmed on the newer smaller dataset received from the currently recruited subjects.
‍
The dataset received was taken through the Data Wrangling process followed by Data Cleaning. The prepared dataset had a good number of missing values.

Considering that a few missing values can be of biological relevance, a threshold of 65% was calculated based on the ratio of missing values across the categorical and numerical columns, and data points with more than 65% missing values were completely removed. This was followed by missing value imputation for 10-15% of the data which remained missing and were technical noise in the data.
‍

Data Cleaning and Further Processes

Data Cleaning was followed by Feature Engineering where numerical columns were subjected to Normalisation (ensuring that no outliers exist) whereas categorical columns were subjected to one hot encoding.
‍
Feature Engineering was followed by model training. Class Imbalance was taken care of by calculating class weights to balance the classes during model training. The best performing ML Model was found to have a specificity and sensitivity of 0.97 and 0.91. K-fold cross-validation was performed to obtain a mean F-1 score of 0.96. Feature significance was obtained using the Ginni index. Top 16 features were selected to re-train the model.
‍
⁠Testing/Prediction accuracy of the model was performed on the new dataset obtained and the F-1 score was found to be 0.95 which further validated the clinical relevance of the model.
