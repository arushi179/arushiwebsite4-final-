WHAT DOES IT DO?

A breast cancer risk predictor is a tool or model used to estimate an individual's risk of developing breast cancer over a specified period, typically within the next 5 to 10 years or over their lifetime.These predictors use various factors and variables known to influence breast cancer risk, such as age, family history of breast cancer, genetic mutations, personal medical history, reproductive history, and lifestyle factors.

FACTORS CONSIDERED

Factors that contribute to breast cancer risk assessment include age, family history (particularly first-degree relatives), genetic mutations like BRCA1 and BRCA2, personal medical history such as breast biopsies, reproductive factors like age at first childbirth, and lifestyle factors such as alcohol consumption, physical activity, and body weight. These variables collectively help healthcare providers gauge an individual's susceptibility to breast cancer.

HOW ITS USED

Healthcare providers utilize breast cancer risk predictors to customize patient care and recommendations. These tools assist in determining appropriate screening protocols (such as mammography or MRI) based on an individual's risk level. They also aid in recommending preventive measures such as lifestyle adjustments.
